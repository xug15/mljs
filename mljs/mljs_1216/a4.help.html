What is the exRpanel-Seeker for Liquid Biopsy ?

  • Circulating extracellular RNAs (exRNAs) have the potential to serve as biomarkers for the liquid biopsy.

  • The exRNAs are existed in body fluids in various forms: bound by proteins forming ribonucleoproteins (RNPs) or lipoproteins (LPPs) complex, or encased within membrane structures like microvesicles (MVs) or exosomes.

  • All known RNA types have been found in body fluids: miRNA, piRNA, mRNA, snoRNA, snRNA, lncRNA, tRNA and Y RNA. However, There still lack an effective tool to fully dig the informative information of exRNAs.

  • The exRpanel-Seeker is based on the extracellular RNA expression matrix, which designed to perform evaluation, prediction, feature selection, which also provide the panel marker information and clinical information.
